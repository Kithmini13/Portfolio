Data warehousing solution based on the Museum of Modern Art Collection dataset

This document outlines the process and architecture for designing a data warehousing solution based on the Museum of Modern Art Collection dataset, sourced from Kaggle. The dataset comprises nearly 20,000 artworks and over 15,000 artists, providing a comprehensive repository of visual art spanning painting, sculpture, photography, and performance art. Due to incomplete information in some records, the data was enriched and organized into multiple customized tables, including Artists, Art Object Details, Subcategories, Date, and Borrowed and Permanent Art Objects. The report details a structured approach, beginning with data set selection and preparation, followed by solution architecture and data warehouse design. It then delves into the Extract, Transform, Load (ETL) process, emphasizing the development of fact tables to support accumulated historical data. This ETL process is enhanced by adding dimensions and hierarchies to enable deeper analytical capabilities. The solution aims to create a robust data warehouse that supports complex queries and analyses across multiple dimensions within the museum's collection data.
